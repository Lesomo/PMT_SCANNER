Laid Metres vs Cut Metres
The most meaningful throughput story — available metres drive cutting output, not just targets
Why this matters: The daily metres available for laying are often less than the planned target due to fabric availability, roll changes, or plan variances. This means the cutting target becomes unachievable that day — not because of poor cutting performance, but because not enough fabric was laid. Measuring cut vs actual laid gives a more accurate picture of cutting room efficiency than cut vs target.

Lean & Theory of Constraints Analysis
Break-even efficiency · idle minute decomposition · TIMWOOD waste · constraint identification
The question this tab answers: The cutting room pays the configured rate per machine-minute regardless of whether the machine is cutting or idle. At what efficiency does the operation actually cover its costs — and of the idle time that isn't covered, what is causing it? The aim is to eliminate waste and unlock the cutting constraint without capital investment. Only 0.4% of production days in 24 months operated at or above normal operation — every other day had identifiable waste.
Break-Even & Cost Floor Configuration
The cost floor is the minimum % of capacity rate that represents your fixed committed costs (labour, overheads, machine lease). Any efficiency above the cost floor generates surplus. Any efficiency below it means you are paying more to run the room than the value it produces. Machine count and rate are set on the Financial tab and apply here automatically.

TOC — Drum, Buffer, Rope Analysis
Drum: The cutting machines — the system constraint. They dictate throughput rate.  |  Buffer: The fabric waiting to be cut — laid metres ready ahead of cutting.  |  Rope: The signal back to laying — how much to lay to keep the drum fed without starving it. Days where laid < target represent the rope being too short, starving the drum.

When laid (buffer) falls short of cut capacity, the drum (cutting) is starved · Gap = constraint starvation · Aim: keep buffer ≥ drum capacity
