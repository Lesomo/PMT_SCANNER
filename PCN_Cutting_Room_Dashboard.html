Operations Overview
22 months · Apr 2024 – Mar 2026 · Received → Laid → Cut → Sorted
Flow summary: Fabric is received, laid (often drawing from stock), cut, then sorted. Laying exceeds receiving by 3.6% avg — operation draws stock regularly. Cutting exceeds laying by 2.4% — carryover fabric. Sorting averages 0.5% loss vs cut (worsening recently). Performance improved 2× from 2024 (31%) to 2025 (64%) — a step-change at Jan-25.

Lean & Theory of Constraints
Waste identification · constraint analysis · Drum-Buffer-Rope · TIMWOOD
TOC Constraint: The cutting operation is constrained by fabric receiving — the operation never approaches its target even when it cuts more than it lays (using buffer). The Drum is the cutting machines. The Buffer is fabric stock. The Rope is the replenishment signal back to receiving. When receiving falls short (May-25: only 125k received), the operation draws buffer — which is healthy until buffer is exhausted.

TIMWOOD Waste Analysis
⏳
Transport
Fabric moves: Receiving → Stock → Laying → Cutting → Sorting
5 physical movements minimum. Buffer stock drawdown (avg 3.6%/month) indicates fabric transit delays between receiving and laying.
📦
Inventory
Buffer stock used as operating mechanism — drawdown in 15/22 months
May-25: laid 135% of received. Cumulative stock balance indicates significant WIP. Carrying cost of unsorted units visible in sort gaps.
🚶
Motion
Sorting losses (avg 0.5%) suggest rework or rehandling
Dec-25 (−31,712 sort gap) and Jan-26 (−26,268) indicate units requiring reprocessing. Floor investigation needed to separate reject vs rework vs timing.
⏸️
Waiting
Primary waste: cutting machines idle when receiving insufficient
Cut never exceeds 540k/month despite targets of 900k–2.5M. The gap between received and target is the dominant waiting signal — estimated 70% of idle time.
⚙️
Over-processing
2024 targets set 2–5× capacity — planning overhead generating wasted effort
Setting and tracking targets at 2.47M when max capacity is 540k creates planning waste. Target recalibration (Jan-25) eliminated this waste.
🏭
Overproduction
Carryover cutting (cut > laid in 13/22 months) can mask receiving shortfall
Cutting from prior buffer creates appearance of full productivity while receiving is under-replenishing stock. Sustainable only while buffer exists.
✂️
Defects
Sort loss worsening: avg 3% loss in H2-25, 9% in Dec-25
Sort÷cut deteriorating from near-100% in 2024 to 85.6% (Dec-25), 90.4% (Jan-26). Defect rate is increasing and warrants root cause analysis.
🧠
Skills
Performance doubled from 2024 to 2025 — step-change suggests capability uplift
30.9% → 63.6% performance is unlikely from target-setting alone. Some combination of training, process improvement, and better target calibration contributed.
